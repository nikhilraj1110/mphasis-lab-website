About the Lab

The Mphasis AI & Applied Tech Lab at Ashoka University advances interdisciplinary research in AI, health, fabrication, and beyond.

Our Mission

The Mphasis AI & Applied Tech Lab at Ashoka University is a collaboration between Ashoka University and Mphasis F1 Foundation, a partnership that has grown stronger since its founding in 2020. The Lab brings together researchers from seven departments — from biology and chemistry to computer science and environmental studies — to tackle problems that no single discipline can solve.

The Lab runs 25+ active research projects across five pillars. BharatSim offers large-scale simulations for epidemic modelling and public health policy. The Centre for Health Analytics Research & Trends (CHART) applies AI at the intersection of healthcare, food systems, and translational medicine — from conversational AI for clinical diagnosis and multimodal food computing to molecular profiling of triple-negative breast cancer. AI@Ashoka pushes the frontiers of AI and Machine Learning across biology, chemistry, environmental science, and more. The Cybersecurity pillar advances post-quantum cryptography research to secure critical internet infrastructure against quantum computing threats.

The Digital Makerspace is a workshop and fabrication lab where students and researchers design, build, and test their ideas — from laser-cut prototypes to custom scientific instruments. From laser cutters and CNC routers to 3D printers and drones, the Makerspace supports both rapid prototyping and scientific instrumentation for research across multiple pillars.

Structure

Five Research Pillars

Our research is organized into five interconnected pillars, each addressing distinct technological and scientific challenges while sharing infrastructure, talent, and cross-disciplinary insights.

AI@Ashoka

AI@Ashoka

Artificial intelligence and machine learning applied across health, biodiversity, education, policy, and materials science. Spanning Computer Science, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Psychology, Environmental Studies, and the Centre for Writing and Communication.

BharatSim

BharatSim

India-scale distributed, multi-scale agent-based simulation framework for epidemic modelling and population health. Led by Gautam Menon and Debayan Gupta, with coverage in 20+ international media outlets.

CHART

CHART

Centre for Health Analytics Research and Trends — AI at the intersection of healthcare, food systems, and translational medicine. Includes folk computing for clinical diagnosis, multimodal food computing, and triple negative breast cancer research.

Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity

Post-quantum cryptography research developing protocols resilient against quantum computing threats. Published at ACM ASIACCS 2025 and advanced research in post-quantum internet security.

Makerspace

Makerspace

Digital fabrication lab, scientific instrumentation, and student innovation hub. Includes the Digital Makerspace, Covalent Magnetic Tweezers, and physics experiments — with publications in Nature portfolio and an enabling role for research across multiple pillars.

History

Our Journey

August 2020
Lab Established

Partnership established between Mphasis F1 Foundation and Ashoka University — creating one of the first corporate AI labs at an Indian liberal arts university.

2020 – 2023
Foundation — Planting the Seeds

5 research projects launched with 5 faculty across AI, epidemiological modelling, and cybersecurity. 9 publications, 57 students engaged. BharatSim simulation framework developed.

2023
NeurIPS Challenge Winners

Mphasis Research Interns win 1st place in Privacy-Preserved Federated Learning and 4th place in the SENSORIUM Challenge at NeurIPS 2023 — the world's top AI conference.

2023
Growth

The Lab expands into five research pillars spanning AI, health, fabrication, and more: AI@Ashoka, BharatSim, CHART, Cybersecurity, and Makerspace.

2023 – 2024
Expansion — Taking Root

Faculty grew from 12 to 19 across 7 departments. Digital Makerspace established with major equipment. 76 students engaged in Makerspace projects. 12 new publications.

2024 – 2025
Maturation — Branching Out

Portfolio expanded to 20+ active projects across all 5 pillars. CHART and Cybersecurity pillars fully operational. First wave of conference presentations and deployed systems.

2025 – 2026
Impact at Scale — In Full Bloom

27+ faculty across 7 departments and 25+ active projects. BharatSim H5N1 study covered by BBC, NDTV, Nature India and 20+ global outlets. Schmidt AI Fellowship at Oxford awarded to faculty. Ministry of Electronics & IT (MeitY) selects project for national AI impact casebook. 42+ research outputs.
