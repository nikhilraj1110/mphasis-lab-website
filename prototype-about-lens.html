About the Lab

The Mphasis AI & Applied Tech Lab at Ashoka University advances interdisciplinary research in AI, health, fabrication, and beyond.

Our Mission

The Mphasis AI & Applied Tech Lab at Ashoka University is a collaboration between Ashoka University and Mphasis F1 Foundation, a partnership that has grown stronger since its founding in 2020. The Lab brings together researchers from seven departments — from biology and chemistry to computer science and environmental studies — to tackle problems that no single discipline can solve.

The Lab runs 25+ active research projects across five pillars. BharatSim builds large-scale simulations for epidemic modelling and public health policy. CHART (Centre for Health Analytics Research & Trends) works on clinical AI, food computing, and cancer diagnostics. AI@Ashoka applies AI and machine learning across biology, chemistry, environmental science, education, and media analysis. The Cybersecurity pillar develops post-quantum cryptography protocols for internet security.

The Digital Makerspace is a workshop and fabrication lab where students and researchers design, build, and test their ideas — from laser-cut prototypes to custom scientific instruments. From laser cutters and CNC routers to 3D printers and drones, the Makerspace supports both rapid prototyping and scientific instrumentation for research across multiple pillars.

Structure

Five Research Pillars

AI@Ashoka AI and machine learning applied across health, biodiversity, education, policy, and materials science. 14 projects spanning seven departments. BharatSim India-scale simulation framework for epidemic modelling and public health policy. Covered by BBC, NDTV, Nature India, and 20+ international outlets. CHART Centre for Health Analytics Research and Trends. AI applied to clinical diagnosis, nutrition and food science, and cancer research. Cybersecurity Post-quantum cryptography research developing encryption protocols that can withstand quantum computing. Makerspace A fabrication lab equipped with laser cutters, 3D printers, CNC machines, and drones. Also home to RedBrick Hacks.

Highlights

Key Achievements

BharatSim

Global Media Coverage — The BharatSim H5N1 avian influenza study was covered by BBC, NDTV, Nature India, and 20+ international media outlets, bringing the Lab's epidemiological modelling work to a global audience.

AI@Ashoka

Schmidt AI Fellowship at Oxford — Faculty from the Lab were awarded the prestigious Schmidt AI in Science Fellowship at the University of Oxford, recognizing excellence in AI-driven interdisciplinary research.

AI@Ashoka

India-AI Impact Summit Compendium — SocraticAI was selected as a case study in MeitY's Compendium on the Real-World Impact of AI in Education.

AI@Ashoka

NeurIPS 2023 Competition Wins — Our Research Interns won 1st place in Privacy-Preserved Federated Learning and 4th place in the SENSORIUM Challenge at NeurIPS 2023.

Cyber

ACM ASIACCS 2025 Publication — Post-quantum cryptography research from the Lab was published at ACM ASIACCS 2025, one of the top venues in computer security.

Makerspace

RedBrick Hacks — The Lab's flagship hackathon series has engaged 430+ participants across multiple editions, fostering innovation and hands-on problem-solving among students.
